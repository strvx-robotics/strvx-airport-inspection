# What to build next 

## Daily self-inspection checklist
This is the core. The airport needs a daily inspection record. Your app should guide the inspector through runway/taxiway/apron/safety-area checks, with pass/fail/notes/photos/issues. Part 139 requires daily self-inspection unless otherwise required by the Airport Certification Manual.

## Unusual condition inspection trigger
This is for weather, construction, flooding, storm debris, surface damage, etc. The app should let ops create a special inspection with reason: “storm,” “construction,” “reported FOD,” “lighting outage,” etc. Part 139 specifically calls out unusual conditions like construction or meteorological conditions.

## Accident/incident inspection trigger
This is already close to what you built: issue → approval → maintenance ticket → repaired → closed. The key is making every record show condition found + corrective action taken. Part 139 requires records of each inspection showing conditions found and corrective actions taken.

## Corrective action records
This means searchable history, PDF/CSV/JSON export, audit trail, timestamps, inspector name, issue evidence, and corrective action history. Part 139 records require 12 months for self-inspection and airport condition dissemination records.

## 12-month record retention/export
This tracks who is allowed to perform inspections, when they were trained, recurrent training dates, and documentation. Part 139 requires qualified personnel, initial/recurrent instruction every 12 months for self-inspection personnel, and training records are retained for 24 months.
